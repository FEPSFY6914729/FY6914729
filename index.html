Introduction

    Technological advancements in robotics, machine learning, and AI-powered systems are transforming industries at an unprecedented pace, 
    leading to significant changes in the job market, work environment, and skill sets required for the workforce of tomorrow (Admin,2025).
    Concerningly, the UK's retail sector, the country's biggest private employer, keeps losing jobs at a fast rate as companies turn 
    to automation and outsourcing to cut costs. The Office for National Statistics (ONS) reported that 2.81 million jobs were lost in 
    September 2024, 40,000 fewer than the previous year and a shocking 225,000 fewer than in 2019 (West, 2024). Automation in retail 
    involves the use of advanced technology and tools to handle manual tasks traditionally performed in physical stores, on ecommerce 
    platforms, and as part of back-end business operations. Use cases include automating customer support and checkout, simplifying return 
    processing, optimizing supply chain management, analysing shopper behaviour, and personalizing marketing campaigns. Through automation, 
    retailers can reduce costs by operating more efficiently, scaling easily to keep up with customer demand, and gaining a competitive 
    advantage by providing a better customer experience (Lynch, 2025).However, job displacement is a significant concern, as many existing 
    retail roles become obsolete or significantly altered (Admin, 2025).

Amazon Go: Why Cashierless Retail Struggled

    A great example is the cashierless amazon go store which was once open in canary wharf; Shoppers could simply grab 
    what they needed, walk out, and have their purchases automatically charged to their accounts - it was an ambitious vision 
    of the future of shopping (Cashierless.com, 2024).  

    For starters, it was incredibly expensive to implement, firstly, each store required a complex network of cameras, 
    sensors,and AI-powered systems to track what customers picked up and automatically charge them. In addition to the                  
    initial setup costs,which could run into the millions of pounds, there were significant ongoing operational costs. 
    Maintaining the infrastructure,constantly retraining AI models, and updating produce catalogues proved to be a                  figure 2
    financial burden. (Cashierless.com, 2024) 

    Secondly, the scalability, While Amazon Go’s technology worked well in smaller, more controlled environments, it struggled to scale for larger 
    grocery stores. The system relied heavily on shelf sensors, which required a fixed store layout. In an industry where product placements 
    are constantly changing, this created a significant problem. Even small changes to the layout could throw off the system, leading to errors 
    in inventory tracking and customer charges (Cashierless.com, 2024). 

    Thirdly, the resistance from customers, for one, the process of downloading an app and linking payment information was seen as a barrier, especially 
    for one-time visitors. Many shoppers were put off by the idea of using an app to gain entry, as it required additional effort compared to simply walking
    into a traditional store. Privacy concerns also played a significant role in limiting Amazon Go’s adoption. The stores relied heavily on cameras and sensors
    to track every movement, raising questions about data collection and surveillance. Shoppers were wary of being constantly monitored, and with Amazon’s 
    reputation for collecting vast amounts of consumer data, the concept was not well tolerated. Although the idea was very complex and saved shoppers time 
    there was serious flaws as to why the shop shut down (Cashierless.com, 2024).

Advantages of Cashierless Stores

    While amazon go highlights the greatest limitations, there are still noteworthy benefits to the rise of automation in retail. 

    The main advantage of shopping at cashierless stores is the speed and convenience it offers. Without the need to wait in line or interact with a cashier, 
    shoppers can get in and out of the store faster. This is especially beneficial for those in a hurry or looking to grab a few items without the hassle of 
    traditional checkout processes (Taylor, 2025). 

    Furthermore, cashierless stores provide a more pleasant shopping experience. Without the stress of waiting in line or dealing with slow cashiers,
    customers can focus on finding the products they need. The convenience of simply walking out of the store once you've finished shopping is a significant 
    draw for many people (Taylor, 2025). 

    Additionally, they capture instore data: every shopping trip generates detailed data about what people buy and how they shop. Retailers gain access 
    to detailed,real-time information about customer behaviour, product popularity, and shopping patterns. Such customer data can be added to the customer’s 
    anonymous profile, enabling the retailer to launch personalised campaigns, engage with the customer after they leave the store, and enhance retention. 
    Retailers can also leverage the data to optimise store layouts,refine product placements, make more informed inventory decisions and conduct predictive
    analysis (Andrea, 2024).
